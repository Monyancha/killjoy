Rental Offer Guidelines ▽

Follow the Guidelines below to ensure your property rental offer listing gets published

Please pay careful attention to the following guidelines before adding any property rental listings.

What you should avoid doing

  1. Defamation of character. Do not attempt to harm the reputation or character of any individual or property through the use of the rental property offer system.
  2. Do not use swearing or degrading terms in any of the sentences used in your offers.
  3. Do not attempt to promote any form of propoganda through the rental offer system.
  4. Do not attempt to attack the religion of an individual or promote your religious views by expressing any religious content in any property rental listings.
  5. No form of pornography or any word related to any pornographic content will be allowed in any property rental listings.
  6. No advertising of any products or any services are allowed in any property listings.
  7. Do not use the response or comment options as an advertising or marketing tool.
  8. Do not attempt to bribe any of the personnel or members of the killjoy.co.za community through the use of the comments or response options.
  9. killjoy.co.za is in part a tool to promote property rental listings, it is not intended to promote holiday or business related destinations including hotels, Inn's, guest houses, bed-and-breakfasts, motels, lodges, resorts, camping sights or any other form of paid temporary accomodation. See a detailed list of properties that should not be added to the listings.
  10. Charity. No form of any charitible cause will be allowed through rental property offer listings.
  11. News through RSS feeds. Do not attempt to synicate your news, weather, weblog or publishing content as an RSS feed.
  12. Dating and chatting. The Internet is abuzz with online datig sites, please avoid using the comments or response options in an attempt to find a match. If it so happens that you accidentally find your long lost love in this manner, we will be happy for you and may even send you flowers for you big day, purely unintentional of course.

What you should be doing

  1. Provide as much detail of the rental property offer as possible.
  2. Update your rental property listing regularly and provide as much feedback as possoble. Make the necessary change when and if some of the property detials have changed.
  3. Delete the rental property listing as soon as you have received a suitable response.
Guidelines for Uploading Photos ▽

Please pay careful attention to the following guidelines before adding any photos to a property review or listing

What you should avoid doing

  1. Defamation of property. Do not attempt to upload any photos thare reflect badly on the image of the property. Any photos that show damage to a property, the state of the property, or any negative illustration of a rental property, will be disregarded.
  2. Do not attempt to hide sentences of afflicting content by means of a graphic illustration or photo.
  3. Profanity. We have a very strict photo profanity policy. No photo's containing cussing, nudity or any other form of vulgarity will be allowed.
  4. Selfies. Don't even try. We are interested in the property behind you ;-)
  5. Advertising. Any photo advertising any product, item or service for sale, will be disregarded.
  6. Personal posessions. Please try your best to avoid animals, cars, clothes on a washing line, kid's toys spread about and other personal posessions in your photos.
  7. Misleading photos. Try your best to add a photo of the property you are renting. Please do not add photo's of properties not related to the property rental offer.
  8. Events. Do not advertise any events through the use of photo's uploaded for a property rental offer.
  9. Malicious content. Do not attempt to upload malicious content in the form of executable files.
  10. Image format. Only jpg, jpeg, gif and png image file types are allowed.
  11. Highlight unique amenities. Promote the details tenants look for in a property.

 

What you should be doing

  1. In a nutshell, only upload frontal, top, side view photos of a property. Interior and exterior photos are what count. Cell phone photos are fine.
  2. Be sure to convert your photos to the jpeg, jpg, gif or png format.
  3. Be careful. Remember that JPEG/JPG photos contain EXIF data. This means that the photo you upload could contain the geodata for the location of where the photograph was taken. If you are uncertain see this article about how to remove exif data from your photographs.
  4. A total of eight (8) photos can be uploaded, with a maximum of 2Mb file size per photo.
  5. Ask permission, if it is not your personal property. Not only is it the right thing to do, but it may avoid conflicts of interests.
  6. See this article for more guidelines on How to make your listing photos look their best.

A very friendly reminder. We reserve the right to remove any photo or group of photos, based on our sole dicretion.
