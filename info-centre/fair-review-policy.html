Fair Review Guidelinse ▽

Follow the Guidelines below to ensure your property rental review or listing gets published

Please pay careful attention to the following guidelines before adding any property rental reviews or listings.

What you should avoid doing

  1. Defamation of character. Do not attempt to harm the reputation or character of any individual or property through the use of the rental property review system.
  2. Do not use swearing or degrading terms in any of the sentences used in your reviews.
  3. Do not attempt to promote any form of propoganda through reviews.
  4. Do not attempt to attack the religion of an individual or promote your religious views by expressing any religious content in any property reviews.
  5. No form of pornography or any word related to any pornographic content will be allowed through any reviews.
  6. No advertising of any products or any services are allowed through the reviews.
  7. Do not use the review response or comment options as a job hunting tool.
  8. Do not attempt to bribe any of the personnel or members of the Rent-a-Guide community through the use of the comments or response options.
  9. Do not attempt to promote your property by adding it to the property listings.
  10. Rent-a-Guide is a community for property rental tenants who wish to share their personal experience with others, it is not intended for review of holiday or business related destinations including hotels, Inn's, guest houses, bed-and-breakfasts, motels, lodges, resorts, camping sights or any other form of paid temporary accomodation. See a detailed list of properties that should not be added to the listings.
  11. Charity. No form of any charitible cause will be allowed through reviews or listings.
  12. News through RSS feeds. Do not attempt to synicate your news, weather, weblog or publishing content as an RSS feed.
  13. Dating and chatting. The Internet is abuzz with online datig sites, please avoid using the comments or response options in an attempt to find a match. If it so happens that you accidentally find your long lost love in this manner, we will be happy for you and may even send you flowers for you big day, purely unintentional of course.
  14. Money. No financial details, including rental price, electricity rates. water tariffs, deposit fees or oustanding rent issues will be allowed through revies.
  15. Finaly, don't get personal. The idea is to get your problems resolved, not to start a war over mundane issues.

What you should be doing

  1. Get to the point. No one has time to read essays of your personal experience of a property. Keep it short and simple and pay attention to keys points.
  2. Be polite. It is the most proven method to deal with any flammable situation. Swearing and insluting others never work.
  3. Pay a compliment where it's due. Certainly there are good aspects about the property. Let others know about it.
  4. Revise your reviews regularly. You may feel different at a later stage and your issues may have been resolved. Update the review in any case.
  5. Provide as much detail of the rental property as possible. Remember that it is your personal experience that help future tenants.
  6. Update your review regularly and provide as much feedback as possoble. Make the necessary change when and if some of your problems have been resolved.
  7. Remember, the rating or your review has a permanent reflection over the overall ratings for a property, Adjust the raring accordingly.
  8. Only you, and no one else, can delete a review. If you feel the property review is no longer necessary, delete it.
  9. Be fair, actions speak louder than words.
Guidelines for Uploading Photos ▽

Please pay careful attention to the following guidelines before adding any photos to a property review or listing

What you should avoid doing

  1. Defamation of property. Do not attempt to upload any photos thare reflect badly on the image of the property. Any photos that show damage to a property, the state of the property, or any negative illustration of a rental property, will be disregarded.
  2. Do not attempt to hide sentences of afflicting content by means of a graphic illustration or photo.
  3. Profanity. We have a very strict photo profanity policy. No photo's containing cussing, nudity or any other form of vulgarity will be allowed.
  4. Selfies. Don't even try. We are interested in the property behind you ;-)
  5. Advertising. Any photo advertising any product, item or service for sale, will be disregarded.
  6. Personal posessions. Please try your best to avoid animals, cars, clothes on a washing line, kid's toys spread about and other personal posessions in your photos.
  7. Misleading photos. Try your best to add a photo of the property you are reviewing. Please do not add photo's of properties not related to the property review.
  8. Events. Do not advertise any events through the use of photo's uploaded for a property review.
  9. Malicious content. Do not attempt to upload malicious content in the form of executable files.
  10. Image format. Only jpg, jpeg, gif and png image file types are allowed.

 

What you should be doing

  1. In a nutshell, only upload frontal, top or side view photos of a property for the property review.
  2. Be sure to convert your photos to the jpeg, jpg, gif or png format.
  3. Be careful. Remember that JPEG/JPG photos contain EXIF data. This means that the photo you upload could contain the geodata for the location of where the photograph was taken. If you are uncertain see this article about how to remove exif data from your photographs.
  4. A total of eight (8) photos can be uploaded, with a maximum of 2Mb file size per photo.
  5. Ask permission. Not only is it the right thing to do, but it may avoid conflicts of interests.

A very friendly reminder. We reserve the right to remove any photo or group of photos, based on our sole dicretion.
